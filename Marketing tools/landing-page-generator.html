The Promise-Led Landing Page Generator

Answer seven prompts about the situation you're writing for. Each one maps to a beat in the Promise-Led framework — recognition, consequence, mechanism, cost, proof, principle, and close. You'll get back a full draft argument, not just a slogan.

Beat 1 of 7 — Recognition

Describe the moment your reader is living in right now.

Not a category or a market — a specific scene. What are they doing, at what time, feeling what, when the problem is most present?

Test: could you picture this exact moment happening to a real person? If it still feels like it could describe any business in any industry, go more specific.

Beat 2 of 7 — Hidden Consequence

What's happening to them that they can't see?

Name the bigger cost behind the daily symptom — the thing they'd be alarmed by if someone pointed it out plainly.

Beat 3 of 7 — The Mechanism

Why is this actually happening?

Give the reader a reason to trust you understand the cause — a model or distinction — without handing over your entire method for free.

Test: could a smart reader now go solve this entirely on their own? If yes, you've said too much.

Beat 4 of 7 — The Real Cost

What does this actually cost, in numbers?

Real figures beat impressive-sounding ones. Rough is fine — just make sure it's derivable, not invented.

Beat 5 of 7 — Proof It's Fixable

What evidence do you have that this actually gets solved?

Real testimonials, results, or examples — the more specific and numerous, the more it reads as a repeatable pattern rather than a lucky story.

Beat 6 of 7 — The Deeper Principle

What's the bigger truth behind this specific problem?

Zoom out from the fix to the worldview. This is the idea that should change how they think, not just what they do next.

Beat 7 of 7 — The Close

What's the one sentence you want them to remember?

Short enough to repeat back from memory. If the six beats above did their job, this shouldn't need to argue anything new.
