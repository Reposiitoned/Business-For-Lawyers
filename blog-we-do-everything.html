Why “We Do Everything” Is the Most Expensive Thing You Can Say

Marketing·1 min read

When prospective clients asked what her firm specialised in, Michelle used to say: “Honestly, we do a bit of everything — conveyancing, wills, family law, small business work. Whatever you need.” It felt generous. It felt like more opportunity.

It also meant every enquiry turned into a negotiation. Prospective clients had no way to judge whether Michelle was excellent at their specific problem, so they judged the only thing they could compare: price. She found herself quietly discounting fees just to win files that should never have needed convincing.

Michelle changed one sentence. When asked what the firm did, she started saying: “We specialise in property and family law for people going through major life transitions — buying a first home, separating, or planning an estate.” The other work didn't disappear. She just stopped leading with it.

People stopped asking for a discount and started asking when she could start.

Saying you do everything doesn't make you more findable. It makes you impossible to distinguish from every other firm saying the same thing.
